Pumps & Motors


Pumps


A pump is a mechanical device for raising water to a higher elevation, creating flow or applying pressure.

There are two types of pumps: Positive Displacement and Centrifugal



Positive Displacement Pumps


Positive Displacement - produces a pulsing flow with a definite volume pumped each stroke.

Positive displacement pumps are primarily used as chemical feed pumps.

They cannot be pumped against a closed valve, or damage will occur to the pump or the water line. These pumps are considered to be self-priming.



Types of Positive Displacement Pumps

  • piston
  • plunger
  • diaphram


Centrifugal Pumps


Centrifual pumps have a rotating impeller within the pump casing.

Water enters at the eye (center) of the impeller.

When the impeller is rotating, water is forced out against the walls of the casing by centrifugal force. The water climbs the inner wall of the casing until it is discharged at the outlet of the pump. It's forced out of the pump by the pressure supplied by centrifugal force from the spinning impeller. A vacuum is created at the eye of the impeller by the water being forced out. This vacuum draws in more water at the suction (inlet) of the pump.


Centrifugal Pump Facts

  • Centrifugal pumps can be pumped against a closed valve

  • They are not self priming. A foot valve must keep water in them, or they must be primed before use

  • The amount of water pumped by a centrigual pump is directly related to the head (water in feet of height) that the pump is pumping against. The higher the head, the less the capacity of the pump will be.

  • If a centrifugal pump cannot supply an adequate discharge head, two or more pumps can be connected in series, with the discharge of the first pump connected to the suction of the second pump. This will not increase the velocity or quantity of water flow, but it will create a higher discharge head (more pressure to raise the water level higher for filling a storage tank).

  • Two parallel pumps of the same capacity pumping into the same pipe will not double the amount of water pumped by a single pump.

  • Pumps should not be installed where they will be subjected to flooding.

  • It is good practice to have two pumps at any critical installation. Alternate pumps to lessen wear.
