MO DNR Study Guide: Regulations


Important Definitions


Primacy – the primary authority for implementation and enforcement of drinking water regulations

Chief Operator – Primary responsibility is the health of the public. If a chief operator quits or is hired it must be reported to DNR within 15 days.

Permit to Dispense – form required before a system can dispense water. Provides a systems 7 digit ID number

Primacy Fee – fee customers pay to offset state lab expenses

Abandoned Well Form – must be filled out when new customers are connected to public system



Colilert Test


This is the primary test used by the State Department of Health in Jefferson City to detect coliform. This test is also called “PA” and “MUG”. It’s the fastest of all tests and takes 24 to 48 hours. The sample is incubated for 24 hours to allow a color change:

     If yellow its coliform positive

     If it Fluoresces under ultraviolet light, it is E. Coli positive

The Sample is held an extra four hours if the yellow color is not determinant (the right shade of yellow) within the original 24 hours. Some Colilert tests require only 18 hours to complete



Membrane Filter Technique


The Membrane Filter Technique for detection of total coliform bacteria is performed only upon request by the State Health Department. The Colilert Test has replaced it.

The Membrane Filter Technique has two Stages:

     1. Presumptive Stage – If coliform is present, the broth will render them a distinguishable metallic green color.

     2. Confirmation Stage – A sample of the bacterial growth though to be coliform is transferred to a Durham Tube. If gas collects in the tube, the presence of coliform is confirmed.

The entire process takes a minimum of 48 hours.



Most Probable Number


This test is also called “MPN” or “Multiple Tube Fermentation”.

The MPN Technique has 3 stages:

     1. Presumptive Stage – Five tubes are used in this test in an incubator at 35° C.

     2. Confirmation Stage – This stage is similar to its counterpart in the Membrane Filter Technique.

     3. Completed – final step to confirm the presence of coliform

The tests are read as “___ colonies per 100ml.” The MPN technique takes up to 7 days to complete.



Level 1 & 2 Assessments


Level 1 assessments are triggered when a system:

     A. Is required to take 40 or more samples a month and more than 5% are total coliform positive

     B. Takes fewer than 40 samples a month and two or more are total coliform positive

     C. Fails to take the required number of repeat samples after any total coliform positive samples in the month


Level 2 Assessments are triggered when a system:

     A. Has an E. coli MCL violation

     B. Has a second Level 1 assessment triggered in a rolling 12 month period

     C. Fails to collect all required repeat samples following any E. Coli positive routine sample.

Any surface or ground water system under the influence of surface water and iron removal or softening plants must collect a minimum of 5 routine samples per month.

Boil Water Advisories


Boil Order – Issued by DNR or Department of Health

Boil Advisory – Issued by a public water supply

Do Not Use Order – Issued by DNR


Public water supplies are expected to issue a Boil Water Advisory under two circumstances:

     1. Confirmed E. Coli found in the water

     2. Pressure less than 20 psi in the system. If this occurs the system must submit a Report of Low Water Pressure to DNR within 48 hours of the occurance.


When Issuing a Boil Advisory:

     1. Contact one television station most viewed by the community

     2. Contact 2 radio stations with the largest local listening audience

     3. Notify your regional DNR office


Boil Water Orders may be issued by DNR for:

     1. Exceeding MCL for finished water turbidity

     2. Disruption in the supply’s disinfection that might result in inadequate bacteria kill

     3. Main breaks that pull a slug of dirt into the distribution system prior to isolation of that section of the system


Boil Orders stay in effect until pressure is restored and until two consecutive special bacT samples show the water is safe to drink.



Public Notification Rule


Violation Tier Reason Response Time
Tier 1 Immediate public health risk 24 hours
Tier 2 Non-urgent health risk 30 days
Tier 3 Monitoring & Reporting Violation 1 year

Tier 1 = Acute health risk
Tier 2 = Non-Acute health risk
Tier 3 = Monitoring or reporting violation

Tier 1 violations require notification within 24 hour via electronic broadcast media sources

Tier 2 violations must be made public within 30 days via direct delivery (mail, newsletter, hand delivery)

Tier 3 violations may be published in the Consumer Confidence Report



Consumer Confidence Report


Consumer Confidence Reports are required to be provided to customers by July 1st each year.

Systems serving 500 people or less may provide direct delivery OR publicly post at water office, library, etc.

Systems serving 500 or more people must provide the CCR via direct delivery AND post it on the internet.



Cross Connections


A cross connection is any connection between a potable water supply and a questionable source.


Types of Cross Connections:

     1. Backflow – a reverse flow due to a customer having a higher pressure in the water line (example: boiler system)

     2. Back Siphonage – a reverse flow due to negative pressure in the water line (examples: fire hydrant pulling water from both directions or a water main break.)


Prevention Measures for Class I Hazards (Health Risks)

     1. Air Gap – This is the most preferred method. It must be a separation distance of at least 2 times the diameter of the discharge pipe above the flood level rim of the receiving vessel. The minimum is 1 inch. All air gaps should be installed above grade where flooding isn’t possible.

     2. Reduced Pressure Principle (“RP” or “RPZ”) – a device that creates a mechanical air gap with its zone of reduced pressure. They must be inspected every year by a licensed inspector. Backflow records are kept by the water system for 5 years.


Prevention for Class II Hazards (Aesthetic Risks):

     1. Double Check Valve. They must be inspected every year by a licensed inspector. Backflow records are kept by the water system for 5 years.

Emergency Response Plan


Under the Federal Bioterrorism Act of 2002, public water supplies serving more than 3300 people must conduct a vulnerability assessment and develop an Emergency Response Plan. This must be updated yearly.
